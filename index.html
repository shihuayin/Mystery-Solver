How to Find Evidence ?

Interview witnesses to obtain firsthand accounts and collaborate with experts like forensic analysts. Utilize technology like UV lights, metal detectors, and chemical tests to uncover hidden evidence. Check for surveillance footage and communication records that might provide insights. Ensure a proper chain of custody by carefully documenting and storing evidence. Preserve trace evidence like hair, fibers, and fingerprints, which could link individuals to the scene.

What does a detective do ?

Detectives employ a range of skills and techniques to conduct their investigations. They meticulously analyze crime scenes, gather and analyze forensic evidence, interview witnesses and suspects, and collaborate with other experts, such as forensic scientists or psychologists. They possess excellent problem-solving abilities, critical thinking skills, and attention to detail to connect the dots and unravel the mysteries surrounding criminal acts.

Four Methods for Finding Fingerprints

Powder, combined with brushes, reveals latent prints by adhering to moisture on friction ridges. Ninhydrin reacts with amines in fingerprints, turning into Ruhemann's purple, aiding detection. Cyanoacrylate fumes react with residues, forming visible prints on non-porous surfaces. Laser or LED devices emit specific light wavelengths, exposing prints that can be enhanced with powders or dyes. These techniques enable crime scene investigators to uncover and enhance fingerprints for identification and forensic analysis.
